# Competitor scan — 2026-05-14

Three AI-video-ad-adjacent sites screenshotted live. Goal: avoid building a hero that's already saturated, find the gap streetwear founders aren't being sold to.

## HeyGen — heygen.com

- **Hero copy:** "Turn your ideas into videos in minutes"
- **Palette:** White background, black headline, electric cyan (#00C2FF-ish) accent on "videos in minutes" + on the primary CTA pill
- **Type:** Big rounded sans (geometric, looks like Inter or similar). No serif anywhere.
- **Composition:** Headline left, three-phone floating 3D-perspective mockup on right (avatar-style talking heads). Subhead is 3-line filler about no cameras, no crew, no editing.
- **Primary CTA:** "Get Started for Free" cyan pill
- **Pattern:** Generic AI-SaaS tool aesthetic. Sells the *tool*, not the *outcome*. Reads as "Loom but for AI talking heads." Zero brand voice.

## Arcads — arcads.ai

- **Hero copy:** "Create winning ads with AI" (italic serif on "with AI")
- **Palette:** Off-white / cream background (#F4F1ED-ish), black + brown text, no chromatic accent
- **Type:** Bold modern serif headline with italic accent. This is the only one with serif in the hero — it's premium-broad.
- **Composition:** Headline center, asymmetric scattered phone-frame mockups around (UGC-style talking head clips). "Seedance 2.0 is live" pill above headline. Two CTAs: dark "Create Your AI Ad" + white "Continue with Google".
- **Pattern:** Closest direct competitor — they use Seedance 2.0 (same Higgsfield model). Positions as "AI Actor library" — a tool you log into, generate ads. Self-serve. Broad market (any DTC).

## InVideo — invideo.io

- **Hero copy:** "CREATE VIDEOS WITHOUT LIMITS" (all caps, oversized, with imagery clipping inside the word "VIDEOS")
- **Palette:** Bright Brand Blue header bar (#2563EB), white body, oversized solid black type
- **Type:** Massive bold geometric sans (chunky display weight, all caps for headline)
- **Composition:** Centered. Subhead under headline ("Turn any idea into videos. Ads, explainers, stories, anything you can imagine.") Big pill input that says "Make an AI explainer video" → "Try invideo" CTA. Below: a tab bar of categories (Effects / Vision / Million Dollar Ads / Money Shot / Performances / VFX House / Advertising).
- **Pattern:** Tool-first, broad market, all things to all people. Feels enterprise. Cookie banner immediately covers content.

## Implications for our positioning

**Avoid:**
- White / cream backgrounds (3 of 3 do this — saturated)
- The "floating-phone-mockup-scatter" composition (3 of 3)
- Generic "Create [X] with AI" headlines (3 of 3)
- Positioning as a *tool* (3 of 3 — we are a *service*)

**Lean into:**
- **Dark background.** Instant differentiation from every competitor. Streetwear shoots in dark too — feels native.
- **Streetwear specificity in copy.** "AI-generated video ads your *streetwear brand* can actually run" — the word "streetwear" appears 0 times across all 3 competitors' heroes.
- **Outcome over tool.** "Three ads, five days, £750." Not "create ads with AI." We don't sell a login.
- **One hero video — not three scattered mockups.** A single 9:16 finished piece, large, doing work — proves we deliver ads, not interface.
- **Honest disqualifier.** Nobody else publicly disqualifies anyone. Doing so (Not a fit if you need real models on camera / pre-revenue / luxury fabric drape) immediately reads as "they actually know who they're for."

**Distinct angle:** We're the *done-for-you streetwear ad agency that happens to use AI*, not the AI ad tool. Arcads is our closest peer — they're a self-serve tool, broad market. We win the streetwear segment by being narrow and finished-product.

## Implication for design directions (Task 3)

Both design directions should be **dark**. The split is:
- **A (editorial):** dark + premium serif + single hero video. Reads as Stripe/Linear for clothing.
- **B (streetwear brutalist):** dark + oversized off-grid type. Reads as Off-White/Stüssy for ads.

Either beats white/cream-SaaS. Pick by which audience tone fits Kyle's voice better.
